Legal and Ethical views on ANN applications

LLMs (Large language models) have been gaining traction over the years for their ability to produce near human responses to queries. They are already business propositions. (Hutson, 2021). Hutson (2021) also mentioned how LLMs were now capable of generating computer code and summarizing legal documents.

Currently, LLMs have started accepting images and documents and can summarize them at electrifying speeds. The possibilities seem limitless.

However, there are limitations currently to the usage of LLMs. Sometimes, LLMs tend to produce responses which exhibit social biases which can spread misinformation. (Chaudhary et al., 2024)

One way to solve this problem as mentioned by Hutson (2021) the best defense currently is to remove toxic text from the training data, but this raises another question. What to exclude.

Another way this could be resolved is by a method proposed by (Chaudhary et al., 2024). The method is called Quantitative Certification of Bias.

Another limitation is the fact that LLMs cannot explain their decision-making process, which makes it difficult to understand how they arrived at a particular solution. (Ufuk, 2023). This affects industries such as healthcare and finance, where accountability is required.

While LLMs have had a significant impact on how to retrieve data, it still requires human judgment to validate if data generated is correct. Currently, it is best to use LLMs to assist human reviewers. (Ufuk, 2023).
