Research Proposal Presentaion

Data visualization from airline sentimental analysis based on reviews on X.

By Asnai Narang

Introduction

Over the years, with the emergence of social media, people have begun to express themselves more openly on social media. Social media now plays a crucial role when people plan their trips and it is a useful tool for companies to advertise their product. (Bigne et al., 2016).

Of all the social medias such as YouTube, Facebook, Instagram, X etc., X (Formerly known as twitter) is one of the purest platforms where people can express their views. (Garg et al., 2020). For the research, the key literature used is Airline Sentiment Visualization, Consumer Loyalty Measurement and Prediction using Twitter Data. (Khan and Urolagin, 2018).

Research Question

The proposed research question is how can airlines use data from social media to improve their opinion amongst customers and also compare where they stand as compared to their competitors?

 

 

Aims and Objectives

The main objectives for the research are to perform sentiment analysis on airline data based on review tweets on X for different airlines and help airlines to track trends on consumer reviews so that it allows them to address the issue consumers are facing.

Ethical consideration

Using data from X may be subject to consent, however if only consented data is used, it may introduce bias in the research being conducted leading to less accurate results. (Allen, 2022). To make sure that data doesn’t breach any regulations, the data needs to be anonymized such that it cannot be traced back to an individual which would violate privacy regulations.

Data Collection

In the research done by Khan and Urolagin (2018), the data collected was done using twitter (Now X) API using the airline help desk handle. In the case that the airline did not have a help handle on X, the official id was used instead. All the data was collected using the Tweepy library in Python. (Tweepy, 2024). The data contained tweets from American, European, Indian and Australian airlines.

Sentimental Analysis

Sentiment analysis helps in extracting the viewpoints and emotions from textual data such as tweets from X. (Higgins, 2022). However, before performing sentiment analysis the data was preprocessed by performing tokenization. (Khan and Urolagin, 2018).

Once the data was cleaned, the sentiment analysis was performed by passing the tweets to the TextBlob library in python. (TextBlob, 2018). This returned the sentiment score between (-1,1). The scores below zero are classified as having a negative sentiment while scores above zero are classified as having a positive score. If the output is equal to zero, it is classified as neutral.

Once the output is obtained for all the tweets, the data can be visualized for different regions from where the airlines belong. Using the mean score of each airline, a stacked bar chart was used to demonstrate how the sentiment of each airline compared to their competitors in the regions.  A line chart was also used to show how sentiments changed over time for airlines with each region represented by a different chart. (Khan and Urolagin, 2018).

Recommendation

While using TextBlob to perform sentimental analysis on the data works, one must also consider other libraries to judge the accuracy of the analysis. As concluded by Mohit (2021), the Vader python library performs better than TextBlob when it comes to negative sentiments. trying out different predictors can significantly enhance the accuracy of the analysis making it more useful to airlines and customers. 

Another recommendation is to perform feature extractions from the dataset to improve predictor performance. As suggested by Krouska, Troussas and Virvou (2016), using appropriate feature selections and representations, the accuracy of sentiment analysis can be improved. 

 

Conclusion

As more people flood the social media with their opinions, it becomes a gold mine for businesses such as airlines to expand their reach to a variety of customers.  They can improve their response time to customers who face issues, can analyze what is the general word around the market about them and can also emphasize on what issues to prioritize. X (Formerly known as twitter) is a social media that is best suited to get public opinions. 

However, data can be quite noisy on social media. To correctly interpret the data, it is important to clean before performing analysis on it. Additional preprocessing such as feature extractions can be used to significantly enhance the accuracy of analysis. Different predicters such as TextBlob and Vader can be used to perform quick analysis, the outcome of which can be converted to various charts like bar, line or pie to help decision makers decide which direction to go. 

These visuals can also help influence customers select which airline to fly with based on recent opinions. A live dashboard which shows the recent sentiment trend by airline would go a long way in helping consumers decide before they plan with whom to fly.
