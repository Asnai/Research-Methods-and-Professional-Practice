Gender Pay Gap in the Technology Sector

By Asnai Narang

 

Introduction

According to Goldin (2014), one of the greatest advances that humanity has made in the last century has been the converging roles and earnings of men and women. Pay for women has significantly improved during the last couple of decades. For example, the pay for women which used to be 59 cents on the dollar in 1970s, is up to nearly 77 cents on the dollar in the United States. (Goldin, 2014). While it is clear that there is still parity between men and women in regards to pay, previous example shows the trend is moving in the right direction to ensure equal pay for all. 

 

 

 

Focus and Aim of the Review

Despite significant advances in technology over the years, pay gap between men and women still exists. (Nacheva and Koleva, 2022). The aim of this literature is to explore the gender pay gap in the technology sector in various countries to understand why women earn lesser than men and analyze the improvements that have been made in the past and what further steps can be taken to achieve equal pay for all.

 

Data Collection

In the research done by (Nacheva and Koleva, 2022), The data was collected in two ways. 

  1. 4185 English publications were taken from twitter (Now X) using phrases such as “IT pay gap”, “IT gender equality” and “IT companies pay gap”. 
  2. Surveying 128 employees in IT companies with 52% being men and 48% percent women. There were 9 questions in the survey related to gender pay gap out of which 8 questions had three possible answers (Yes, no, I Don't know). There was one open ended question which allowed the participant to express their opinion on gender pay gap.

 

Results

The outcome of the research (Nacheva and Koleva, 2022) was that in most cases, companies do provide equal pay to men and women but women still suffer from pay inequity. While the pay gap is still there between men and women, the gap is narrowing in younger generation. (Eurostat, 2022). 

Why women earn lesser than men

In the European Union, the pay for women was 12.7% less per hour compared to men. (European Commission, 2022). Some of the causes due to which women earn less than men as stated by European Commission (2022) are: 

  1. Sectoral segregation: Due to overrepresentation of women in low paying sectors such as healthcare and education, the pay for women is less. According to Kulik (2024), women are employed at a high rate in education sector and low rate in technological sector.
  2. Unequal share of paid and unpaid work: women spend more hours on unpaid work which hampers their career choice as compared to men.
  3. The glass ceiling: There are less women working at the top of hierarchy which influences the level of pay. 
  4. Pay discrimination: Women are paid less for doing the same work as men by employers which can be constituted under sexual discrimination.

There were other factors mentioned by Aragão (2023) for women earning lesser than men such as women feel more pressure to focus on responsibilities at home which leaves them with less time to focus on career.

Aragão (2023) also stated that becoming parents can decrease the pay for women. 

Religion can also be a factor that affects pay for women. For example, Jewish women devoted more hours towards work as compared to Arab women since Arab women experience higher work-family conflict compared to Jewish women. (Kulik, 2024)

Actions taken to promote equal pay

  1. The United Nations has set an agenda for sustainable development by 2030. One of the goals is to ensure equal pay amongst men and women. (United Nations, 2015).
  2. The European parliament added the principle for equal pay as an important aspect of the principle of equal treatment between men and women. (Anon, 2011).

Recommendations to improve equal pay

There can be many ways to improve the pay gap between men and women. One way is to ensure the work demands do not invade personal life (Kulik, 2024) which allows women to have better work life balance.

Another way could be to encourage pay transparency so that lower pay employees can negotiate for higher pay. (Cullen, 2024)

Conclusion

Gender pay gaps has been widely discussed during the last couple of decades and the efforts have paid dividends over the years. Even though the pay gap still exists, one of big positives is that the pay gap is narrowing with younger people. (Aragão, 2023). This literature highlights the causes as to why women are paid lesser to men along with actions that have been taken to address the issues and what further steps can be taken to decrease the pay gap between men and women.
