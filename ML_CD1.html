The 4th Industrial Revolution

According to Schwab (2016), the technological revolution the way humans live unlike anything mankind has ever seen before. The speed of transformation already has no historical precedent which is disrupting almost every industry in every country.

With such significant growth, the impact of technological failures can have devastating consequences. For example, the crashes of two Boeing 737 max planes in which the root cause was determined to be the MCAS (Maneuver Characteristics Augmentation System).

To make the 737 MAX behave similar to previous generations, Boeing had installed the MCAS software to help pitch nose down in case the nose pitched up too high. But faulty sensor data caused the nose to pitch down which led to the crashes of two planes in the span of a few months. (Holeman, 2020).

This caused significant damage to Boeing’s safety reputation and they had to pay significant fines as a result. (Holeman, 2020)

Cases like this highlight why technology should be used responsibly. Legislators and regulators need to cope with the rapid pace of change and the impacts that can occur due to the fourth industrial revolution. (Holeman, 2020).
