Individual reflection

As I reach the end of Research Methods and Professional Practice, I look back to reflect on the knowledge I gained as a person.  This has been the most challenging course I have faced during my masters' studies although a lot of this has been due to external circumstances which significantly impacted my learning. 

As soon as the course started, my professional workload increased drastically. At first, I thought I could manage it but by the end of 1st month, it became clear that I won't be able to provide enough time for the course. I reached out to student support asking if I could withdraw from the course to which I was told it was too late to withdraw and retake it later.  So, I decided to power through and try my best for the first assignment. 

It was not good enough and was correctly pointed out by my tutor who cited lack of enough references. The workload was too much and I faced similar problems during my research proposal. 

To make matters worse, there was a medical emergency in my family which meant I had to quit my job and go abroad urgently that caused me to miss my deadline for final submission. 

I was asked to resubmit all the assignments and did the best I could although I would have preferred to take the course from scratch again so that I could learn the right way. 

Regarding the things that I learnt, I can confidently say that I have improved my researching skills significantly through this course. I learnt about different types of research methods such as conclusive and descriptive research. I also learnt different data collecting methods and the advantages and disadvantages to different data collection methods.

The statistical assignments in unit 7 to 9 were particularly helpful as they helped me expand my knowledge regarding statistics and also enhanced my excel skills in analyzing data and creating charts. 

As for things I can improve on, I have to work on my time management and be more spontaneous in taking decisions. Managing time efficiently, I can complete tasks faster and leave time for more work. (Houston, 2023)
