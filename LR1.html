 Mobile and E-Learning in Nigerian Higher Education

 

By Asnai Narang

 

Introduction

According to Oyelere et al. (2016), Mobile and E-learning is playing a very important role in improving the teaching and learning process globally by advancing accessibility and engagement between students and teachers. The traditional method was more focused on teachers as compared to students while the new E-Learning system is designed to assist more towards learners. (Eze et al., 2020). However, implementing E-learning in the Nigerian higher education system faces several challenges such as infrastructure limitations. 

 

Purpose

The purpose of this study is to understand the benefits of E-Learning and analyze the challenges of implementing the E-Learning systems in Nigerian Higher educations and provide solutions to help overcome them.

 

Data Collection

For this study, literature based on well-structured Questionnaires were used to help analyze the difficulties of implementing the system in Nigeria and recommendations on how those issues can be resolved.

 

Benefits of E-Learning

Based on the Survey, 93 percent of students said that M-Learning (Mobile learning) helped motivate them and was useful for their studies. (Oyelere et al., 2016).

 

Challenges limiting E-Learning adoption in Nigeria

According to Oye, Salleh and Iahad (2011), Internet connectivity was a major issue. High internet costs made it challenging for students to afford internet. Also, Nigeria has poor internet connectivity which makes E-learning hard to embrace. (Oyelere et al., 2016). Unreliable electricity in Nigeria also disrupts the adoption of E-Learning which is due to lack of financial assistance to enhance infrastructure to enable E-Learning. In a survey conducted by Oyelere et al. (2016), 96% of respondents highlighted inadequate infrastructural development as factors for low adoption. 

Oyelere et al. (2016), also cited Security concerns and emphasized the need to protect sensitive data along with providing secure access to learning platforms.

Another issue identified by Oyelere et al. (2016) was the low computer literacy. They cited that the lack of digital skills can limit the use and benefits of E-Learning system. 

 

Solutions

To overcome the challenges stated above, multiple changes are required. For Nigeria, UNESCO recommended 26% of the annual budget towards education and advised the government to improve the electricity supply in the country. (Oyelere et al., 2016).

To address the technical literacy issue, significant training programs are required to enhance the digital skills of both teachers and students. (Onyema, 2020)

 

Conclusion

To conclude, e-learning shows significant promise for Nigerian education system. However, there are significant challenges that currently limit the potential and adoption of E-Learning in Nigeria.

  1. Reliable internet access is crucial for e-learning platforms to function effectively. Nigeria needs to invest significantly in its internet and electricity infrastructure to make sure E-Learning is available and accessible to everyone. 
  2. The cost to implement E-Learning can be very high for institutions as it includes processes such as employee training and infrastructure installation which hinders the adoption of E-Learning. Significant funding is required to help institutions with adoption of E-Learning along with subsidized data plans for students to make internet affordable. 

 

This literature review highlights the research based challenges of E-learning adoption in Nigerian education system along with recommendations on how to overcome them.
